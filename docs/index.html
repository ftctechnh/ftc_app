Our Members

Team 6504, Torque Omega, is from Westlake High School in Austin, Texas. Although the team has competed before, the current team members are for the majority new to robotics. Despite the fact that most of our team has little to no prior experience, we've been extremely successful in competition, development, and design this year.

Eli Bradley

Eli Bradley is a freshman at Westlake High School, and one of two programmers on our team. This is his first year of involvement in the FTC program. He joined our team because he wanted to see how standardized vehicle development works. Eli is interested in a career in software engineering, and likes to practice software development in his free time.

Megan Cox

Megan Cox is a sophomore at Westlake High School. Megan has been doing robotics for three years, this is her second year in FTC. Megan joined robotics because she likes to build robots, and wants to pursue a career in STEM. In her free time Megan likes to work on her dad’s car with him, practice the harp, and play video games.

Daniel Descant

Daniel Descant is a sophomore at westlake High School. Daniel likes to build things. This is Daniel’s first year in ftc but 4th year in the robotics program. Daniel’s job on the team is to design the shooting mechanism. He is looking into pursuing a career in stem, possibly for the military.

Alya Mansoor

Alya joined robotics because she enjoys partaking in building and programming robots. In her free time, Alya likes to watch YouTube videos, play video games, and make pieces of art.

Edward Martinez

Edward Martinez is a sophomore at Westlake High School. He is participating on the robotics FTC team for the second year and is on the intake sub team. Edward joined robotics because he is wanting to be an engineer when he gets older. In his free time he races and plays video games.

Sean Miller

Preston Williams

Preston Williams is a freshman at Westlake High School. This is his first year being involved in FTC, and is on the peripheral scoring sub-team. Preston joined robotics to learn more about how hardware and software come together to create something better. Preston is not sure exactly what he wants his future career to be, but he is interested in computer engineering. In his free time, he likes to watch movies or play video games.

Mentor Team

The mentors for Torque Omega in 2016-17 are Bert Bradley, Wagner Williams, Mansoor Ejaz, and Nick Leclair. Each is involved in the tech industry in different roles. There are software and hardware engineers, as well as project managers and other roles. Each mentor has brought valued experience and advice to the team this year, while allowing the students to perform their own work.
